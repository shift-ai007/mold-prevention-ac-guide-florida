# Mold Prevention Checklist for South Florida Homeowners

A monthly and seasonal checklist to keep your HVAC system operating as an effective mold barrier.

## Monthly Tasks

- [ ] Pour 1 cup of distilled white vinegar down the condensate drain line
- [ ] Check for water stains near supply registers and on ceilings below attic ductwork
- [ ] Verify thermostat is set to AUTO fan mode (not ON)
- [ ] Check indoor humidity with a hygrometer — target 45–55%
- [ ] Inspect visible ductwork connections for condensation or moisture

## Every 60–90 Days

- [ ] Replace air filter (MERV-11 to MERV-13 recommended for South Florida)
- [ ] Check filter housing seal — gaps allow unfiltered humid air to bypass the filter
- [ ] Inspect drain pan under indoor air handler for standing water or algae

## Twice Per Year (Spring and Fall)

- [ ] Schedule professional AC maintenance and coil inspection
- [ ] Have technician check refrigerant charge (low charge = poor dehumidification)
- [ ] Inspect attic ductwork insulation for damage, gaps, or compression
- [ ] Check all duct connections and joints for air leaks
- [ ] Clean supply and return register covers
- [ ] Test system cycle times — runs shorter than 10 minutes suggest oversizing

## Annually

- [ ] Professional duct cleaning and inspection (every 3–5 years in Florida, annually if issues detected)
- [ ] UV-C lamp replacement if installed (bulbs lose effectiveness after 12 months)
- [ ] Check and clean blower motor and fan blades
- [ ] Verify attic ventilation is adequate (reduces duct condensation)

## Warning Signs — Act Immediately

If you notice any of these, schedule a professional inspection:

- Musty or earthy smell from any vent
- Visible mold on walls, ceilings, or near registers
- Persistent condensation on windows despite AC running
- AC running constantly without achieving comfortable humidity
- Allergies or respiratory symptoms that improve when leaving the house
- Water dripping from air handler or visible in drain pan overflow

## Humidity Reference Table

| Indoor Humidity | Status | Action |
|----------------|--------|--------|
| Below 40% | Too dry | Rare in South Florida — check for duct leaks pulling in dry outdoor air |
| 40–55% | Ideal | No action needed |
| 55–60% | Caution | Monitor closely; check filter and drain line |
| Above 60% | Mold risk | Schedule professional inspection immediately |

## Emergency Response

If you discover active mold growth:

1. Do not disturb the mold (spreading spores makes it worse)
2. Increase ventilation in unaffected areas
3. Do not turn off the AC — it is still dehumidifying
4. Contact a licensed mold remediation company for assessment
5. Have your HVAC system inspected for the moisture source

---

*This checklist is designed for the South Florida climate (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach counties). Homes in other regions may have different maintenance intervals.*
